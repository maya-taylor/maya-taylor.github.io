Arduino Shield for UBC Rocket Payload

Project Description:

The Arduino Shield for UBC Rocket Payload is a critical component designed to manage voltage readings, temperature sensors, and data logging within the Microbial Fuel Cell (MFC) payload of the UBC Rocket 2024 project. It ensures vital systems monitoring during the rocket's flight. The shield was designed in AutoDesk Fusion and features LED indicators for data writing status and a safety switch for SD card power control. Temperature sensor and fuel cells are connected to the board via screw terminals. Regular updates and revisions have been made to optimize performance and fit within the rocket's payload bay. Documented iterations of PCB drafts and Bill of Materials (BOM) updates track the project's progress. Key considerations include component placement, ground connections, and power supply options. The Arduino Shield project demonstrates practical engineering application in aerospace contexts, emphasizing problem-solving and collaboration. It contributes to advancing scientific exploration and aerospace technology.
